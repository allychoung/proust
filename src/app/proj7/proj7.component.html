Conclusion

September 9, 2023

A poem I wrote with the members of the Creative Writing Group


As a child you played with ant hills

          sprawled on all fours

while your mother fawned and cooed

          and your aunt looked on from atop a swinging chair


You stepped outside every morning

          to note that the porch seemed smaller, smaller

and the wooden planks receded into the rotted foundation

          creaking with every step


At night your mother ran her fingers through her hair

          collecting oils—fine and sweet—

and the pipes squeaked while the water ran down, down

          over slender hairs pooling atop the drain


From the porch you viewed a distant mountain

           spanning beyond the ends of the porch

its face grinned at yours

           grinning back.
